Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore was a great poet, philosopher, and freedom thinker of India. He was the first Indian to win the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913 for his book Gitanjali. Tagore wrote poems, songs, stories, and plays that touched people’s hearts with their simplicity and depth. He also composed India’s national anthem, Jana Gana Mana. Apart from literature, he founded Visva-Bharati University to promote education based on creativity and culture. Tagore is remembered as a universal figure who spread the message of peace, love, and humanity.
